C.04.2 General handling rules for Swiss Tournaments (effective from 1 February 2026)

This version updates the general handling rules for Swiss tournaments, taking effect on 1 February 2026. The structure is similar to the earlier rules but uses numbered articles.

1 Pairing systems

Published systems. A FIDE‑rated Swiss tournament must use one of the published Swiss systems. Accelerated methods may be used if announced in advance and listed among the FIDE‑approved accelerations.

Unpublished systems. A system or acceleration not published by FIDE may be authorised provided that a detailed written description is submitted to the Qualification Commission and temporarily authorised, and that it is included in the tournament regulations and presented to the participants before play.

Chief arbiter’s declaration. When reporting a tournament, the Chief Arbiter must declare which official Swiss system and acceleration method were used, or supply the temporary authorisations.

No preferential pairings. Swiss systems must pair participants objectively; pairings must not be altered to favour any player.

2 Initial order and late entries

Measure of strength and TPN. Each participant is assigned a measure of strength (usually their rating). For pairing purposes they are ranked by (1) strength, (2) FIDE title and (3) alphabetically. Tournament pairing numbers (TPNs) are assigned accordingly, and adjustments may be made until after the fourth round.

Late entries. Players entering late are paired only from the round when they appear; they receive no points for earlier rounds.

3 Pairing, colour and publishing rules

Adjourned games and withdrawals. A postponed game counts as a draw for pairing purposes. Participants who have withdrawn or are known not to play a round are not paired in subsequent rounds.

Colour sequence. Only games actually played count toward a player’s colour history. If two participants were paired but did not play, they may be paired again later.

Pairing order. Pairs are sorted using the criteria defined in the FIDE (Dutch) System: the pair containing the player on the highest score is listed first; within equal scores, pairs are ordered by the sum of the players’ scores and then by the smallest TPN.

Correction of pairings. If a pairing error is reported before the next round, pairings may be corrected with minimal disruption. Once a round has started, results remain valid.

The 2026 rules retain the same underlying principles as the earlier version but provide clearer numbering and emphasise objective and reproducible pairings.
