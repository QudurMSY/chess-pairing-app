C.04.4.1 Dubov System (effective till 31 January 2026)

The Dubov Swiss system is a FIDE‑approved pairing method aiming to make Swiss tournaments fairer by equalising each player’s average rating of opponents (ARO). The version described here was valid until 31 January 2026.

Preface and principles

Fair treatment. The Dubov system seeks to ensure that players with the same score face opponents of similar average strength. It does this by matching players with low ARO against those with high ratings.

Requirement for ratings. Every player must have a rating; unrated players cannot be paired using this system.

Initial ranking and TPN. Players receive tournament pairing numbers (TPNs) following the general handling rules. They are sorted by rating, title and alphabet, and may be rearranged until after the fourth round.

Definitions

Scoregroup and bracket. As in other Swiss systems, a scoregroup contains players with the same score. In the Dubov system there are no downfloaters: players from a higher scoregroup are never moved down to a lower one to make pairings.

Colour preferences. Colour differences and preferences follow the general rules (absolute, strong, mild and none). The Dubov system emphasises alternating colours where possible.

Average rating of opponents (ARO). For each player, ARO is the average of the ratings of all opponents faced so far, excluding games that were forfeited. When a player has played fewer than two games, the ARO is considered undefined and the player may receive provisional treatment.

Maximum upfloater. A formula determines how many players from a lower scoregroup can float up to pair with higher‑scoring players. The aim is to minimise differences in ARO.

Pairing procedure

The Dubov system pairs players as follows:

Form scoregroups. Players are ordered by score and TPN. Within each scoregroup, players are sorted by ascending ARO (lowest ARO first). This ensures that players with weaker opposition thus far are considered first for pairing with stronger opponents.

Pair by ARO. Players with low ARO within a scoregroup are paired against players with high ratings in the same group. If the group has an odd number of players, an upfloater from the next lower scoregroup is selected based on maximum upfloater rules to create an even bracket.

Assign colours. Colours are allocated using the standard colour‑preference hierarchy.

Repeat for subsequent scoregroups until all players are paired.

The Dubov system’s distinctive feature is the ordering of players by ARO within each scoregroup and the avoidance of downfloaters. These choices aim to produce more balanced tournaments where players with equal scores have faced opponents of similar average strength.

C.04.4.1 Dubov System (effective from 1 February 2026)

From 1 February 2026 the Dubov system was slightly restructured, with clearer numbering while retaining its core aim: to equalise the average rating of opponents (ARO) faced by players in the same scoregroup.

0 Preface

The system is designed to give each player in a scoregroup an ARO as close as possible to those of their peers. Ratings are mandatory; tournaments using this system must ensure all players have a rating.

The system uses tournament pairing numbers (TPNs) as defined in the general handling rules.

1 Definitions

TPN and initial order. Players receive TPNs and are initially ranked by rating, title and alphabet. Rearrangements may be made until after the fourth round.

Scoregroups and brackets. Scoregroups contain players with the same score; brackets are even‑sized groups to be paired. Unlike other systems, the Dubov system does not create downfloaters.

Colour difference and preferences. Colour differences (White minus Black games) determine absolute, strong and mild colour preferences, as in other Swiss systems.

Average rating of opponents (ARO). For each player, ARO is computed as the average of the ratings of all opponents faced, excluding forfeits. Players with fewer than two games receive provisional treatment.

Maximum upfloater. A formula determines the number of upfloaters permitted from lower scoregroups to ensure fairness.

2 Pairing principles

Order of players. Within each scoregroup, players are sorted by ascending ARO. Those with a lower ARO are paired against those with higher ratings, to equalise average opposition.

No downfloaters. Players are never moved down to a lower scoregroup; only upfloaters are used to create even brackets.

Colour allocation. Colours are assigned according to absolute, strong and mild colour preferences as defined in the basic rules.

3 Pairing process

Create scoregroups based on current scores and TPNs.

Sort each scoregroup by ARO, lowest ARO first.

If the group has an odd number of players, select one upfloater from the next lower scoregroup, respecting the maximum upfloater limit.

Pair players within the group, matching low‑ARO players with high‑rated players, and assign colours according to preference.

Repeat for each scoregroup until all players are paired.

By using ARO to order players and avoiding downfloaters, the 2026 Dubov system continues the goal of balancing average opposition strength within each scoregroup.
