C.04.4 Other FIDE‑approved pairing systems (effective till 31 January 2026)

This short subsection notes that FIDE recognises a small number of alternative pairing systems besides the Dutch system. Their use is deprecated unless organisers employ a FIDE‑endorsed program containing a free pairing‑checker. Without such a program, organisers are advised not to use these systems. The main alternatives are:

Dubov System – a pairing method focusing on equalising the average rating of opponents (see C.04.4.1).

Burstein System – a method that seeks to maximise fairness by balancing opposition indices within scoregroups (see C.04.4.2).

Lim System – an older pairing method approved in 1987 and amended in subsequent years (see C.04.4.3).

To protect fairness and reproducibility, FIDE encourages arbiters to use standard Swiss systems or official software when employing these alternative methods.
