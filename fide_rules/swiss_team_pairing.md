C.04.6 Swiss Team Pairing System (effective till 31 January 2026)

The Swiss Team Pairing System adapts the general Swiss rules for team competitions. The version described here was in force from 1 August 2024 to 31 January 2026.

Preface

The individual Swiss rules (basic rules and general handling rules) apply mutatis mutandis to team events, except that Articles 6 and 7 of the basic rules (dealing with individual colours) do not apply.

Colours are less important in team events because teams often consist of an even number of players and players can be substituted or shifted between boards. Therefore, colour preferences are weaker and tailored for team competitions.

Key features

Team composition and board order. Teams consist of a fixed number of boards. Players may be rotated between boards within limits set by the event regulations.

Scoring. Standings are typically determined by match points (2 points for a win, 1 point for a draw, 0 for a loss) and sometimes by game points. Tie‑breaks may use the sum of opponents’ match points, game points or other criteria.

Pairing rules. Teams are paired based on match points and, if necessary, game points. Pairings follow Swiss principles: teams do not meet twice, and pairings are objective and reproducible. Colour allocation (which team plays White on the odd‑numbered boards) follows a simplified preference order because colours are less critical for teams.

Byes. If there is an odd number of teams, one team receives a bye worth two match points. The bye is normally given to the lowest‑ranked team that has not yet received a bye.

Publication and correction of pairings. Pairings are published before each round. Corrections are allowed if errors are found prior to the start of the round, but results stand once play has begun.

The team pairing system thus adapts Swiss tournament principles to accommodate match‑based scoring and flexible colour assignment, ensuring fairness while recognising the different nature of team competitions.

C.04.6 Swiss Team Pairing System (effective from 1 February 2026)

From 1 February 2026 the Swiss Team Pairing System remains largely the same but is restated with clearer numbering. It modifies the individual Swiss rules for team competitions.

0 Preface

The basic Swiss rules and the general handling rules apply to teams with the exception that Articles 6 and 7 of the basic rules (dealing with individual colour alternation) do not apply.

Because teams can rotate players and usually have an even number of boards, colour assignments are less important; therefore, colour preferences for teams are weaker and of different varieties.

1 General principles

Match scoring. Teams earn match points (often 2 for a win, 1 for a draw). Tie‑breaks may use game points or opponents’ match points.

Pairing order. Teams are ranked by match points and, if necessary, game points. The pairing process mirrors the individual Swiss pairing procedure: teams with equal points form scoregroups and are paired within those groups.

Colour assignment. In team events, the colour refers to which team has the white pieces on the odd‑numbered boards. Absolute, strong and mild colour preferences are defined similarly to individual events but are weaker because colours matter less.

Bye assignment. If there is an odd number of teams, one team receives a bye worth two match points. The bye is given to the lowest‑ranked team that has not yet received a bye.

Publishing and correcting pairings. Pairings are published prior to each round and may be corrected if errors are found before play begins. Once the round is underway, results stand.

By adapting the Swiss system to team competitions and softening colour preferences, the 2026 team pairing rules seek to maintain fairness while recognising the flexibility inherent in team events.
